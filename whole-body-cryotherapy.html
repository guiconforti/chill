What is whole body cryotherapy?

Whole body cryotherapy is a short cold-exposure treatment, usually about three minutes, where your body is surrounded by very cold air inside a cryo chamber. At ChillRx Delray, people use it to support muscle recovery, ease soreness and inflammation, and get a noticeable lift in energy, mood and skin. You step in, you step out, and you get on with your day.

Who it's for

Whole body cryo is for athletes and weekend warriors recovering from training, anyone managing day-to-day aches and inflammation, people who want an energy and mood boost, and those working on skin tone and overall wellness. It pairs especially well with red light, the infrared sauna and hyperbaric oxygen.

Benefits

  • Supports faster muscle recovery and less soreness after training
  • Helps calm inflammation and everyday aches
  • Often leaves you with a clear-headed energy and mood lift
  • Can support skin tone and a refreshed appearance
  • Quick — about three minutes, with no downtime

What to expect

  1. Check in. A team member greets you and goes over anything you need to know — first-timers are walked through every step.
  2. Step in. You enter the chamber in minimal clothing with protective gloves, socks and slippers provided. Your head stays above the chamber.
  3. Three minutes. Cold air surrounds your body for about three minutes while a team member stays with you the whole time.
  4. Step out and go. You warm back up quickly and head straight back to your day — many members do it on a lunch break.

Results & timeline

Many people feel an immediate lift in energy and reduced soreness the same day. Recovery and inflammation benefits tend to build with consistency, which is why members often come in two to four times a week around training or busy seasons. Results vary by person.

Risks & considerations

Whole body cryotherapy is well tolerated by most healthy adults and sessions are brief and supervised. It may not be appropriate during pregnancy or with certain conditions affecting cold tolerance, circulation, blood pressure or nerve sensation. Share your health history with the team, and check with your physician if you have any concerns. This service is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure or prevent any disease.
